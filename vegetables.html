VEGETABLES

CABBAGE

FUN FACTS

The scientific name of the cabbage is Brassica oleracea var. capitata.Cabbage is from the family Cruciferae.Cabbage is considered Russia’s national food. Russians eat more than seven times as much cabbage as the average North American.Cabbage is available in many varieties: red or purple, green and Napa cabbage, usually found in Chinese cooking.Cabbage is low in saturated fat and cholesterol.Cabbage is high in dietary fiber, vitamin C, vitamin K, folate, potassium, manganese, vitamin A, thiamin, vitamin B6, calcium and iron.One cup of cabbage is about 15 calories.Drinking juiced cabbage is known to assist in curing stomach and intestinal ulcers.Cabbage contains quantities of fiber and iron, which help keep the digestive tract and colon in a healthy condition.

TOMATO

FUN FACTS

The scientific name of the tomato is Solanum lycopersicum.There are around 10,000 varieties of tomatoes worldwide.The largest ever tomato on record was picked in Oklahoma USA, in 1986. It weighed over 3.5kg. La Tomatina is an annual festival in Spain, where people throw 150,000 tomatoes at each other.tomatoes are not always red. Instead, they can come in a variety of colours including yellow, pink, purple, black and even white.Tomatoes are the richest source of lycopene available to us naturally. Lycopene is a very powerful antioxidant which is important for the health of the prostate gland in men. In Ohio, USA, they love tomatoes so much that tomato juice is the official state drink.

CUCUMBER

FUN FACTS

The scientific name of the cucumber is Cucumis sativus.Cucumbers originate from south East Asia.Cucumbers can be up to 2 foot long!China produces 76% of cucumber a gherkin production in the world.A raw cucumber is actually 95% water.A whole cucumber is just 16 calories.Cucumbers can be grown in soilless medium, such as in water(hydroponic). Cucumbers do not provide a lot of nutrition, but the green peel contains vitamins A and C.

CARROT

FUN FACTS

The scientific name of the carrot is Daucus carota.Carrot gets its orange color because of its beta-carotene content.Carrots are made up of 88% water! They have 7% natural sugars, and the rest is protein, fiber, ash, and fat.Carrots are so rich in nutrients that one medium-sized carrot can cover your kid’s daily serving of vegetables.A city in California called Holtwille is called the ‘Carrot Capital of the World’ and has been conducting an annual carrot festival every year for at least the past 60 years.

CORN

FUN FACTS

The scientific name of the carrot is Zea mays.The types of corn grown in America are dent corn, flint corn, pod corn, popcorn, flour corn, and sweet corn.91 gallons of water is needed to produce one pound of corn.Usually corn is yellow, but it can also come in colors such as green, red, or white.While many consider corn a vegetable, it’s actually both a grain and a fruit.It consists primarily of insoluble fiber, which makes it a low-glycemic index food. This means it is a food that is digested slowly and doesn't cause an unhealthy spike in blood sugar. It also contains many B vitamins, as well as essential minerals, including zinc, magnesium, copper, iron and manganese.

BROCOLI

FUN FACTS

Broccoli is an annual or biennial vegetable belonging to the cabbage family.The word “broccoli” comes from the Italian plural of broccolo, which means “the flowering crest of a cabbage”, and is the diminutive form of brocco, meaning “small nail” or “sprout.”Broccoli has been around for more than 2000 years.There are 34 calories in 100 grams of Broccoli.Broccoli is known as the “Crown of Jewel Nutrition” because it is rich in vitamins and minerals.It is an excellent source of vitamin K, vitamin C, chromium and folate. Broccoli is a very good source of dietary fiber, pantothenic acid, vitamin B6, vitamin E, manganese, phosphorus, choline, vitamin B1, vitamin A.The health benefits of broccoli include its ability to prevent cancer, improve digestion, lower cholesterol levels, detoxify the body, and maximize vitamin and mineral uptake.

MUSHROOM

FUN FACTS

Mushrooms are neither plants nor animals. They’re a fungus and belong to kingdom fungi.Mushrooms are the fruiting bodies or reproductive structures of a much larger underground fungus. The sunlight is not needed in mushrooms to make energy for themselves.Mushrooms contain around 90% water.Different species of edible mushrooms are a great source of vitamin B, essential minerals like potassium and copper. In mushrooms, salt content, carbohydrates and fat are very low. Therefore, they are considered a very nutritious food.China is the country where edible mushrooms are produced in the largest quantity in the world.A single Portabella mushroom which is a species of mushroom can contain more potassium than a banana.

POTATO

FUN FACTS

A potato is a root vegetable. It’s a tiny plant with much foliage.The Incas of Peru were the first to cultivate potatoes around 8000 BC to 5000 BC.The scientific name of the potato is Solanum tuberosum.The edible portion of the potato is a tuber that grows underground.There is a lot of starch and other carbs in a potato. If the potato is exposed to light, the tuber will turn green and become toxic.

FUN FACTS

The scientific name for bell peppers is Capsicum annum.Red bell peppers have more than twice the vitamin C of a green pepper. Bell peppers are a good source of Vitamin A. Peppers were named by Spanish explorers searching for peppercorn plants to produce black pepper.
